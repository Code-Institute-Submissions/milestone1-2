Art Gallery

Art has played a part of most of my life, there's never a moment when I'm outdoors something catches my eye and I think how I can make something with it. The Highland landscape is one of my favourite subjects and features a lot in my work, but I'm always looking at new subjects and styles to experiment with so watch this space for any new developments!

Castle Gòrach

Castle Gorach

Pastel on paper

Castle Gòrach is a ruined folly on Tulloch Hill, north of Dingwall in the Scottish Highlands. It was built by the Davidson family, the then estate owners of the area.

Castle Gorach is one of my favourite subjects as well as being curious, it is on my doorstep, and crops up in my work from time to time. This particular piece depicts the castle in the early 1990s as the trees growing from it were removed by vandals in the early 2000s.

Clach MhicLeòid

Clach MhicLeòid

Pastel on paper

Clach MhicLeòid (MacLeod's stone) is a neolithic standing stone on a Hillside at the end of Tràigh an Iar near Horgabost on the west coast of the Isle of Harris, overlooking the Isle of Taransay. The stone get's it's name because it was used as a rallying point by the MacLeods of Harris.

This piece was a commission I did for a friend who originally hails from nearby Leverburgh in Harris. It, along with the many beaches in the area of which Tràigh an Iar (West Beach) is a spectacular example.

Dunnottar at Sunrise

Dunnottar at Sunrise

Pastel on paper

Dunnottar Castle is a ruined medieval fortress on a rocky headland on the Aberdeenshire coast near Stonehaven. The current buildings date around 1400-1600 CE and have played a prominent part in Scottish History including being the hiding place of the Scottish Crown Jewels from Oliver Cromwell's army in the 17th Century.

This piece was a gift to a friend who hails from Aberdeenshire and it seemed an appropriate subject, I wanted to capture the castle early morning as the sun rises from the North Sea.

Kilchurn Castle

Kilchurn Castle

Pastel on paper

Kilchurn Castle is a ruin on the north east end of Loch Awe in Agyll. It was built in the mid 14th Century as the seat of the Campbells of Glenorchy.

I wanted to capture the castle and surrounding landscape in Autumn here, which is the most stunning season for landscape of this type and throughout Scotland. Scottish castles on a lochside have inspired artists throughout the ages and I am no different, Kilchurn is a spectacular example.

Lùib

Lùib

Pastel on paper

Lùib Cottage from the roadside near Achnasheen. Lùib was the childhood home of my late mother and I wanted to capture it as it was then, not an easy feat as the building is now modified and the walls now rendered.

A gift for my late mother who grew up here as her father was a railway ganger and this was one of the many railway cottages. I wanted to capture the cottage with the mist covered hills towering above it in the background.
